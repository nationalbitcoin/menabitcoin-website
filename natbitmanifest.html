Fiat currencies have stopped to perform the functions of money.

Fiat (fiduciary) currencies, which have served as money for half a century, are a tool of policy, not money.

People don't notice it due to habit, but fundamental problems exist. Fiat currencies are constantly being devalued. Bitcoin have not become money and turned out to be centralized contrary to its original philosophy.

It is necessary to take into account mistakes and this time decentralize the new Bitcoin not only in the software code, but also OUTSIDE the protocol.

Ensuring the implementation of purely peer-to-peer cash requires not only technological, but also socio-cultural and ideological breakthroughs.

Decentralization through national borders

Bitcoin networks need to be introduced at the national and regional levels when National Bitcoins of a single network are distributed among citizens of several neighboring states with close ties, who are competing for each other's markets and economically forming a potentially self-sufficient territory. Regional "walls" will reduce the pace of growth of the global oligopoly on the new Bitcoin and may even prevent it. The division into many independent territorial Bitcoin networks is also useful in the context of the scalability problem.

Of course, the network's software protocol knows no borders. It is extremely difficult to organize administrative control to create and maintain separate territories. But this is not necessary: if at least 80% of all coins are released in the first block and immediately distributed to residents of a certain territory, the border of the area will be formed on its own. Along with people's movement, borders will gradually erode, but this will not turn off the main effects of such an act of division into currency zones.

Decentralization through mass coin distribution.

At least 80% of the National Bitcoins of each network must be distributed for free at the very beginning on a self-sufficient territory to as many people as possible, as quickly as possible, and equally. The remaining 20% of the emission at most goes to pay for the functioning of the network (mining or its equivalent). A quantum computer-resistant and energy-efficient consensus mechanism already exists..

Mass distribution has deep economic meaning. People who live in territories they can protect, where they produce goods and services, trade and exchange them, are the original source of money, not some abstract emission procedure imposed by a handful of politicians and bureaucrats. Because National Bitcoins are fully distributed among the people, those who usually had the advantage of proximity to the monetary "printing press" do not receive any undeserved privileges.

Free distribution provides clear legal justification for the origin of funds. All National Bitcoins initially arise within a mechanism understandable to everyone.

Network structures gain value and importance with a large number of nodes. The presence of the same accounting unit for a large number of people allows this unit to become money.

Implementation. The Role of the National Bitcoin Community.

The undeniable advantage of local complementary currencies in times of crisis undermines the authority of central power, which is dangerous. For example, in conditions of crisis and unemployment during the Great Depression, the complementary currency of the Austrian town of Wörgl revived production and domestic demand, while the rest of the continent continued to sink into economic chaos. The "Miracle of Wörgl" inspired six neighboring towns to copy its monetary system, and Eduard Daladier (Prime Minister of France) even visited there to see the currency in action. It was precisely the resounding success of the experiment and the fact that more than two hundred neighboring towns were already preparing to reproduce it that led to the currency of Wörgl being declared illegal. It should be noted that the immediate return of social despair and economic depression served as a breeding ground for the emergence of Nazism.

It is worth considering the modern experience of the credit system of the Swiss business cooperative WIR, which has remained in harmony with the authorities for decades (since 1934). WIR operates as a commercial credit union through which tens of thousands of small and medium-sized enterprises can access financing in the form of WIR francs at an interest rate of less than 1%. This currency, issued in the form of loans, CANNOT BE CONVERTED into regular money or devalued. Stodder and Lietaer's research (2016) shows that this system has a stabilizing effect on macroeconomic cycles. During periods of economic boom, small businesses usually prefer bank loans, while in periods of decline when bank interest rates rise, they turn to WIR loans. There are other similar successful examples such as IRTA, Sardex, RES, PuntoTRANSacciones, Banco Palmas, Bangla-Pesa.

So, the rule is as follows: it is necessary to avoid conversion..

Conversion (currency exchange) is not a strict necessity. Although among enthusiasts of community and territorial currencies there are always many dreamers seeking to overcome poverty and deprivation and who are critical of earning money for its own sake, the main reason for turning to National Bitcoins will be an economic shock on the demand side, leading to low turnover and unemployment. Therefore, first of all, participants in the system need to buy goods from each other, within the territory.

Price stability is a key factor in economic development. Price jumps prevent people from seeing real market signals and supporting efficient resource allocation. While smooth changes do not hinder economic vision, suddenness and volatility worsen the situation.

But it is necessary to restrain not the nominal end prices, but the root cause of unnecessary speculation. It is necessary to build as short closed supply chains as possible.
